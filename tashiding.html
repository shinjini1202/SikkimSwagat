Tashiding Monastery

Tashiding Monastery, founded in the 17th century, is regarded as one of the most sacred monasteries in Sikkim where devotees believe that visiting and offering prayers can cleanse one’s sins and bring blessings.

Name

Tashiding Monastery

History

Built in 1641 by Ngadak Sempa Chempo Phunshok Rigzing, a follower of Guru Padmasambhava, Tashiding is one of Sikkim’s holiest monasteries. It belongs to the Nyingma sect of Tibetan Buddhism. The name “Tashiding” means “The Devoted Central Glory”, symbolizing its spiritual importance.

Inside the Monastery

The monastery houses sacred Buddhist relics, old scriptures, and beautiful frescoes. The main attraction is the Thongwa Rangdol chorten, believed to wash away sins.

Fun Facts

  • Tashiding is said to be blessed directly by Guru Padmasambhava himself.
  • From the monastery, you get panoramic views of Mount Kanchenjunga.
  • It is one of the four most sacred Buddhist pilgrimage sites in Sikkim.

Festivals

The monastery is famous for the Bumchu Festival, with masked dances and rituals.where a sacred pot of holy water is opened annually to predict Sikkim’s future. Losar (Tibetan New Year) is also celebrated here with rituals and prayers.
