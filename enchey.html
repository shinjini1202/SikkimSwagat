Enchey Monastery

Enchey Monastery, originally a small hermitage later rebuilt in 1909, is surrounded by spiritual legends of tantric powers and is celebrated for its annual Cham mask dances that attract both locals and tourists.

Name

Enchey Monastery

History

Enchey Monastery was built in 1909 under the reign of Sikkim’s Chogyal, though a smaller hermitage stood there earlier. The site is believed to be blessed by Lama Drupthob Karpo, a tantric master famous for his flying powers. It follows the Nyingma sect of Tibetan Buddhism.

Inside the Monastery

Inside, the monastery has images of Guru Padmasambhava, Lord Buddha, and deity Loki Sharia. Colorful murals, thangkas, and protective deities’ masks add to its spiritual aura.

Fun Facts

  • “Enchey” literally means “The Solitary Monastery.”
  • The site is believed to be spiritually powerful and free of evil spirits.
  • Its annual Cham dance festival draws locals in vibrant traditional attire.

Festivals

The monastery is famous for theDetor Cham Festival, the triumph of good over evil. Losar (Tibetan New Year) is also celebrated with rituals and prayers.
