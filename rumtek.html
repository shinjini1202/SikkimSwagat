Rumtek Monastery

The largest monastery in Sikkim, Rumtek is the center of the Kagyu lineage and features stunning Tibetan architecture.

Name

Rumtek Monastery

History

Rumtek Monastery, also known as the Dharma Chakra Centre, was originally built in the mid-1700s by the 9th Karmapa. It fell into disrepair until the 16th Karmapa rebuilt it in the 1960s after coming to Sikkim. Today, it serves as the main seat of the Kagyu sect of Tibetan Buddhism in India.

Inside the Monastery

The monastery houses a treasured collection of rare Buddhist scriptures, ancient manuscripts, sacred murals, and exquisite thangka paintings that narrate episodes from Buddhist teachings. Its interiors are richly decorated with vibrant colors and symbolic motifs, creating a deeply spiritual atmosphere. A major highlight is the magnificent golden stupa, which enshrines the relics of the 16th Karmapa and serves as a focal point of devotion for monks and pilgrims alike.

Fun Facts

  • It is the largest monastery in Sikkim and one of the most significant outside Tibet.

Festivals

The most important festival celebrated here is Losar (Tibetan New Year), with masked dances and rituals. The Mahakala Dance, performed during the Tibetan lunar new year, draws huge crowds of devotees and tourists.
